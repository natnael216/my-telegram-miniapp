Newton's Laws of Motion

1. Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia)

An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

2. Newton's Second Law (Law of Acceleration)

The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass. This is often written as: F = ma, where F is force, m is mass, and a is acceleration.

3. Newton's Third Law (Action and Reaction)

For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. This means that forces always occur in pairs.
